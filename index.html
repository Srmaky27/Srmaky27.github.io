- The Sun -

The sun is a massive, luminous ball of gas that is the center of our solar system.

It is a yellow dwarf star with a diameter of about 1.4 million kilometers and a mass of approximately 1.99 x 10^30 kilograms. The sun provides warmth and light, and its powerful gravitational force keeps the planets in orbit around it. It is composed primarily of hydrogen gas and helium, and temperatures at its core reach up to 15 million degrees Celsius.

The sun plays a crucial role in the survival of life on Earth, providing the energy necessary for photosynthesis and regulating our planet's climate.
